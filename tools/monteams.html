Monteams: a practical Pokemon team builder

A browser workflow for turning six Pokemon into a team with visible roles, coverage, and tradeoffs.

What Monteams is for

Monteams is a free online Pokemon team builder for players who want to test a team before committing to it in a game. It keeps the six-Pokemon plan easy to review while you compare cores, partners, weaknesses, and coverage.

The useful question is not whether a tool can generate a perfect team. It is whether you can see why a team works, where it is fragile, and which slot should change. Monteams is designed around that planning loop.

Open the Monteams team builder

A repeatable planning workflow

  1. Choose the game first. Available Pokemon, moves, abilities, and rules vary by generation.
  2. Pick a core. Begin with a favorite Pokemon or a pair whose roles complement each other.
  3. Add answers to weaknesses. Look for safe switch-ins, not only another attacker.
  4. Check role overlap. A team with six similar attackers can lack speed control, utility, or defense.
  5. Review the final six together. Check repeated weaknesses, missing offensive answers, and fragile dependencies.

Type coverage is a decision aid

Type coverage is most useful when it explains a choice. If several team members share a weakness, the next addition should address it or provide a reliable way to play around it.

The Monteams type coverage guide is a useful companion for reading coverage results as tradeoffs.

Generation-specific team planning

A story-mode team, a random battle team, and a competitive team have different priorities. The generation-specific team builder guides give you a starting point for games across the series.

Why browser-first matters

Team planning often happens while comparing a favorite Pokemon or discussing a battle. A no-install browser tool keeps that loop short: open the builder, test a change, inspect the result, and continue.
